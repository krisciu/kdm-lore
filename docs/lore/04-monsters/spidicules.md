# Spidicules

> *"Vibrating to make itself invisible to the human eye, the Spidicules leaves its forest dwelling and lurks in settlements unnoticed."*

**Category:** Monster  
**Type:** Quarry (Node 2)  
**Expansion:** Spidicules Expansion 1.6

---

## Overview

The Spidicules is a nightmare given form—a spider-like abomination that has evolved beyond the need for stealth in shadow. Through an obscene vibration of its entire being, this creature renders itself invisible to human perception, allowing it to walk unseen among the very survivors it hunts. Where other monsters announce their presence with roars or the trembling of stone, the Spidicules simply *is not there*—until it is too late.

A Node 2 quarry monster, the Spidicules represents a pivotal threat to expanding settlements. It fills an ecological niche similar to the Screaming Antelope, offering survivors new tools and avenues of growth through the harvesting of its valuable silk. Yet the price of such resources is paid in terror and blood, for this creature does not merely kill—it *takes*. Survivors vanish from their lantern-light, spirited away to nourish an ever-growing brood of hungry young.

The horror of the Spidicules lies not only in its predation but in its patience. It lurks, it watches, and when it strikes, entire settlements may not realize they are being harvested until the screaming begins.

## Physical Description

The Spidicules possesses a form that blasphemes against natural order—multiple legs splayed in configurations that suggest both spider and something far more ancient, tipped with spindly fingers that grasp and clutch with terrible purpose. Its body emanates a greasy miasma, an oily corruption that hangs in the air and marks its passage for those with senses keen enough to detect it.

Most disturbing is the creature's capacity for vibration. Through some unknowable biological mechanism, the Spidicules can oscillate its entire form at frequencies that render it invisible to human eyes. Survivors have reported the sensation of being watched, of feeling breath upon their necks in empty rooms, only to discover themselves alone—or so they believed. This invisibility is not perfect concealment but rather a predator's ultimate ambush tool, allowing the Spidicules to study its prey, to learn their patterns, before selecting its victims.

The creature's legs serve dual purpose: locomotion and lethality. Hit-and-run tactics define its hunting strategy, darting in to strike before retreating beyond reach. Only by hacking off these limbs can survivors hope to slow its relentless harassment and force a conclusive engagement.

## The Brood

Perhaps more terrifying than the Spidicules itself is what follows in its wake: legions of Spiderlings that respond to their progenitor's phenomenal call. These lesser creatures mass steadily throughout any prolonged encounter, an army of chitinous hunger that grows with every passing moment. The Spidicules need not defeat its prey directly—it need only distract and delay until its children number in the thousands.

The relationship between parent and offspring follows no sentiment. The Spiderlings are voracious beyond reason, and should the Spidicules fall in battle, its own brood will turn upon the corpse with the same hunger they direct at human flesh. This cannibalistic tendency speaks to the creature's fundamental nature: the Spidicules is not a being but a process, a mechanism for converting life into more of itself, endlessly, ravenously.

Survivors who hunt this creature must harvest its valuable silk quickly, ending the showdown before they are overwhelmed and devoured by the swarm.

## Hunting Behavior

The Spidicules does not hunt for sustenance alone. Sources indicate it kidnaps survivors "for its own delight" as well as to feed its brood—suggesting a sadistic intelligence behind those vibrating, invisible eyes. It leaves its forest dwelling specifically to lurk in settlements, watching, waiting, selecting.

What happens to those taken alive remains mercifully unclear. What is known is that the Spidicules maintains its brood, nourishes it, grows it ever larger. Each survivor dragged into the darkness represents not merely a single death but countless future Spiderlings fed and strengthened.

## Resources and Significance

Despite its horrors, the Spidicules offers expanding settlements crucial resources. Its silk is valuable beyond measure—strong, versatile, and harvestable only from those brave or desperate enough to face the creature and its brood. The Aggregate Silk Gland represents a particularly potent organ, suggesting the creature's silk-production capabilities are centralized and can be claimed by those skilled enough to extract them.

## Lore Significance

The Spidicules embodies one of Kingdom Death's most primal fears: the predator that cannot be seen, the horror that walks among us undetected. While other monsters are forces of overwhelming violence or cosmic wrongness, the Spidicules is intimate in its terror. It knows your settlement. It has watched you sleep. It has counted your children.

Its role as a Node 2 quarry places it as a mid-tier threat that settlements must overcome to continue growing—yet its unique mechanics of invisibility, hit-and-run tactics, and the ever-present Spiderling swarm make it a fundamentally different challenge than the Screaming Antelope it replaces. Where the Antelope is prey that fights back, the Spidicules is a predator that has merely allowed you to believe you are the hunter.
