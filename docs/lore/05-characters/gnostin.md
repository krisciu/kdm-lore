# Gnostin

> *"He prays not for salvation, but for silence. The darkness has promised him nothing—and that is everything he desires."*

**Category:** Character  
**Type:** Survivor / Hunt Leader  
**Expansion:** Vignette of Death: Screaming Nukealope

---

## Overview

Gnostin is a weary survivor who has gazed too long into the abyss of existence in the Kingdom Death universe and found himself wanting only one thing: an end. Unlike so many who cling desperately to life in the endless dark, Gnostin has embraced the inevitability of death and actively prays for its arrival. His death wish is not born of cowardice, but of a profound exhaustion—a soul worn thin by the horrors of survival and, perhaps most unbearably, by the relentless optimism of his companion Ashbloom.

Despite his longing for oblivion, Gnostin possesses a cunning mind and natural leadership that draws others to him. He leads a "close knit company" of survivors—a squabbling, unlikely alliance bound together not by hope, but by Gnostin's singular vision. That he continues to fight, to lead, to *survive* despite his yearning for death speaks to either a cruel irony of fate or perhaps a final purpose he must see fulfilled before the darkness claims him.

## The Unlikely Alliance

Gnostin's company is described as "squabbling" and "unlikely"—a ragtag band of survivors who have rallied around a man who wishes he were dead. This paradox defines their dynamic: followers who believe in their leader's vision, even as that leader's ultimate vision is his own demise. Among this company is Ashbloom, whose unflagging optimism serves as a constant source of torment for Gnostin. Where Gnostin sees the futility of existence, Ashbloom apparently sees light. This friction is noted as "just one of many reasons" Gnostin prays for death, suggesting his burdens are manifold.

The alliance's cohesion comes from "wits and teamwork" rather than brute strength, indicating Gnostin values cunning and cooperation over raw power. They have taken it upon themselves to hunt the Screaming Nukealope—a creature transfigured by radiation whose very existence is constant pain.

## The Perfect Ending

Gnostin pursues what is described as his "envisioned perfect ending." Given his death wish, this perfect ending likely involves a meaningful death—perhaps taking the Screaming Nukealope with him, or ensuring his company survives even as he falls. The hunt against the Nukealope represents either a suicide mission dressed as heroism, or the one final act that would give Gnostin's tortured existence meaning before it ends.

The choice of the Screaming Nukealope as his final quarry carries symbolic weight: a creature in constant agony facing a hunter who craves the release of death. In destroying it, perhaps Gnostin sees a kindred spirit—or a mirror of what he might become if death continues to elude him.

## Lore Significance

Gnostin represents the darker psychological spectrum of survival in Kingdom Death. While many survivors are defined by their struggle *against* death, Gnostin is defined by his struggle *toward* it. He embodies the philosophical question at the heart of the setting: in a world of perpetual horror, is survival truly victory, or merely prolonged suffering?

His relationship with Ashbloom creates a compelling dichotomy—the death-seeker and the optimist, bound together in the darkness. That Ashbloom's hope is explicitly cited as a reason for Gnostin's despair suggests the cruelty of mismatched perspectives among survivors.
