## Screaming God Update

[IMAGE]

Production of the Screaming God expansion has entered its next stage. We have just settled on the final sizing and tolerances for the product after working closely with our manufacturing partners. This part of the process generally involves reviewing blank samples and pre-production samples of the product alongside a whole bunch of dieline adjustments and iteration. Though it is not the most engaging work, it's absolutely pivotal for ensuring that nothing is damaged or misaligned when the product is being shipped for fulfillment.

Now that input from our end has mostly wrapped, there will likely not have any substantial updates on the Screaming God Expansion until we have production samples in hand. I do not have a definite estimate from our partners yet on when they will arrive, but there'll likely be some pretty cool pictures once we receive them!

## Expansions of Death Painting Contest

[IMAGE]

Now that we have cleaned up and streamlined our contest logistics, I am happy to unveil the winners of our Expansions of Death Painting Contest! As is always the case, the Dark Judges and I were absolutely blown away by the incredible talent on display from our community of passionate fans. You'll find a showcase of the winning entries here, while the full online gallery should be available on our site in the coming month.

I've also confirmed with our logistics team that our new workflow should allow for more rapid distribution of non-bespoke pieces of prizing, so entrants can expect to see their store credit and King's Coin rewards available in time for next month's annual Black Friday bonanza! Further correspondence will be sent out in the coming weeks to 1st place winners to provide additional details about their contest exclusive prize model.

Thank you again to everyone who shared their love for Kingdom Death by submitting their wonderful work!

## Diorama

A brief interjection here! Due to a lot of confusion during the blind judging process with identifying the specific categorization of each diorama, the Dark Judges were unfortunately unable to delineate between the originally proposed 3 categories for diorama entries. As a result, judgment was rendered upon the Diorama category as a whole rather than by the original categories.

In order to ensure that there is no reduction in prizing due to this fact, and to reward all of the entrants went above and beyond in creating such elaborate works, all winners of the Diorama category will reach prizing equivalent to achieving 1st Place in the category ($100 Store Credit, King's Coin, and a Exclusive Miniature), while all other diorama submissions will receive double the originally outlined participation prize as well as a Honorable Mentions ($30 Store Credit and a King's Coin). Thank you everyone for your understanding!

## Honeycomb Weaver Expansion

[IMAGE]

In the spirit of Halloween, I thought it would be a ripe opportunity to provide a slice of insight on the Honeycomb Weaver, a monster (and expansion!) I have held very close to my chest up until this point. I have proclaimed my love for the Titan Bee many times in the past, and in truth I am just as excited for the other monsters in this box. 

A major source of inspiration for these fastidious monsters is the storied genre of JHorror. Kingdom Death has always ascribed to its own brand of uncanniness and horror, but it has been immensely satisfying process for us to use the lenses of KD to filter the ideas we love from classic pieces of horror media into something uniquely macabre. 

I'll have more to share on the Honeycomb Weaver in future updates as we approach closer to its release, but I am beyond excited for players to experience what horror looks like in Kingdom Death when we REALLY lean in. 

[IMAGE]

Oh also in wonderful news, all of the hard plastic for this set has been finished! A grand total of three armor sets (Titan, Steel Wax, and Rawhide Wax) and three monsters (including the younger version of the Weaver)!

## KDS KICKSTARTER THIS BLACK FRIDAY?!

Who can say! We are still very undecided! I would very much like to expand that team, increase its stability and realize more of the features we are excited to make faster! But I just don't know... would it be weird to put a cap on it? So the spots are limited and aimed more towards people that are passionate about supporting more of what goes into software development and less of a "general audience type of campaign"? We are still mulling it over! 

We are slow and while the team on the SIM is different then the team that's finishing up our long awaited Kickstarter promises, I would not want to make any of you guys here feel betrayed or that our attention is some how divided. So, feel free to be open and honest (and respectful) about the subject in the comments below! 

I worry that, if we don't move on it now - we may lose a really great opportunity to give the Dark Architects more firepower! 

Thanks!!
